How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

Following specifically the Agile-Scrum methodology, the first step, before sprint 1, is discussing with the clients, and understanding what they are expecting from the product. 
After this initial meeting we can then break up their requests into the what, why and overall value provided of each feature. Creating our User Stories. 
Whenever you are able to visualize all the requests as simple phrases it makes it that much easier to realize the goal and then begin creating the solution. 
Multiple meetings with the clients may needed to fully realize the requests, but that's okay. Our goal in tackling any project is to create the best program we can while ensuring the client is more than satisified.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member goes way beyond an individuals performance.
A good team member, specifically in software development, is a member that contributes to the success of the project and the overall health of the team. A team's success is rooted in the individuals being able to come together, work harmoniously, and to help when they can. Each member needs to be set in the teams goal and standards. I know we all have plenty of stories about team members who brought the team down, not necessarily because of performance, with their attitudes toward the overall end goals or not wanting to contribute the same amount of effort. 
A team is only as good as its weakest link. Many hear that and think only of performance quality, but it also means the individuals willingness to help and be present in the team in their struggle for greatness.
